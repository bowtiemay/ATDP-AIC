Starting by learning Python somewhat in-depth from my brother, I then did surface-level stuff with C#/Unity and JavaScript.

I took TIC later that summer, where I learned HTML and CSS (duh), albeit perhaps not to professional standards, caused by my procrastination.

However, it was my first 'real' class in computer science, so I finally have a grade backing up my DIY CS education.

In preparation for the placement test for AIC, I went from not touching code all school year to screwing around with Discord bots and webscrapers. Again, nothing too complex.


If someone off the street asked me what kind of programmer I am, I would say "full stack".

In reality, the "stack" I know is more like "a couple object-oriented languages (mainly python) along with basic html/css".

I know nothing about connecting the two together. That's what I've been told AIC is for, though.

So, in short, I'm excited to try to attempt to live up to my ego!
